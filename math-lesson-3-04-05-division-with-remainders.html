Today's Mission
Divide when the total doesn't split evenly. Find the remainder.
❓
Essential Question
What if it doesn't split evenly?

14 ÷ 4. You can make 3 groups of 4 (that's 12), but 2 counters are left over. Those 2 are the remainder — written as R 2.

14 ÷ 4 = 3 R 2.

📐 Key Concept

Division with remainder:

total ÷ divisor = quotient R remainder

The remainder must always be SMALLER than the divisor. If it equals the divisor, you can make one more group.

Worked Example: Sushi Plates
Problem

23 pieces of sushi. Each plate holds at most 3. How many plates do you need?

A
Atlas — start
"23 ÷ 3. Skip-count by 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21. That's 7 plates with 2 left over."
H
Hazel — the math
"23 ÷ 3 = 7 R 2. Seven full plates and 2 pieces leftover."
F

"But we need ALL the sushi on plates. Those 2 extras need a plate too. So 7 + 1 = 8 plates."
C

23 ÷ 3 = 7 R 2 → need 8 plates

When the story asks "how many plates/buses/boxes," ROUND UP if there's any remainder. The extras still need somewhere to go.

Problem
