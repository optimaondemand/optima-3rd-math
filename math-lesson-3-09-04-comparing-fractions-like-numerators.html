Today's Mission

Essential Question

Vocabulary

When fractions have the same numerator, you have the same number of pieces. To compare, look at the denominators — the one with the smaller denominator has bigger pieces, so it is larger.

🍫

How the Two-Strip Comparison (same numerator) works. Pick the denominator (how many equal parts the strip is cut into). Pick the numerator (how many parts to shade). Read the fraction below.

📐 What You Just Saw

Worked Example: Comparing 3/4 and 3/8
Problem

Atlas ate 34 of a sandwich. Hazel ate 38 of the same-size sandwich. Who ate more?

A
Atlas — count the parts
"Atlas cut his sandwich into 4 pieces. Hazel cut hers into 8 pieces. Same numerator: 3 pieces each."
H
Hazel — count the shaded
"Atlas's 4 pieces are bigger than Hazel's 8 pieces because his sandwich is split fewer ways."
C
Cora — write it
"3 big pieces > 3 small pieces. Atlas ate more."

Atlas ate more: 34 > 38
