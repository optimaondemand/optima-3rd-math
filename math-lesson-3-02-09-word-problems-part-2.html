A student's work

47 − 28 = 19. Atlas has 19 leaves.
⚠️
The fix
Atlas has MORE than Hazel. So Atlas's number should be BIGGER than 47 — not smaller. The student subtracted when they should have added. The correct math is 47 + 28.
47 + 28 = 75. Atlas has 75 leaves.
✏️ What "sense check" would have caught this mistake right away?
📐 Key Concept

Three habits of a good word-problem solver:

1. Cross out the extras.   2. Draw a bar (or sketch the picture).   3. Sense-check the answer.

If the question asks for the BIGGER amount, your answer should be bigger than what you started with. If smaller, smaller. One quick look catches most mistakes.

📋
Three Quick Sense Checks
What's the answer SUPPOSED to look like?

Before doing any math, ask: is the answer bigger or smaller than what I started with?

StorySense checkReal answer
Felix had 62 marbles. He bought 18 more. How many now?Bigger than 62 (he got MORE).80
Hazel had 92 cookies. She gave 35 away. How many left?Smaller than 92 (she gave away).57
Cora scored 25. She scored 17 FEWER than Felix. Felix's score?Bigger than 25 (Felix is the MORE one).42
🔍

The sense check happens BEFORE the math. If your final answer breaks the sense check, you used the wrong operation.

Worked Example: The Extra Number
Three facts. Two matter.
Problem

A bakery sold 156 muffins on Friday. They sold 89 muffins on Saturday. The bakery has been open for 12 years. How many muffins did the bakery sell in both days combined?

A
Atlas — read like a detective
"I see three numbers: 156, 89, and 12. The question asks about MUFFINS from Friday and Saturday. The '12 years' tells me how long the bakery has been open — that has nothing to do with the muffins. So 12 is the EXTRA number."
H
Hazel — draw it
"I'll use a part-whole bar. Friday muffins = 156 (one part). Saturday muffins = 89 (other part). Both days combined = the whole. To find the whole, I ADD the two parts."
F
Felix — the math
"156 + 89. I'll break apart 89 into 80 + 9. 156 + 80 = 236. 236 + 9 = 245. So 245 muffins in two days."
C
Cora — sense check
"Sense check: I added two amounts, so the answer should be bigger than either one. 245 > 156 and 245 > 89. ✓ It makes sense. And reverse-check: 245 − 89 = 156 ✓. Two-day total: 245 muffins."

156 + 89 = 245 muffins

💡

The "12 years" was a trap. It's a real number, in the same problem — but it doesn't answer "how many muffins?" Skip it.

A

Word problem

The school garden has 175 plants. 80 plants are sunflowers. 30 students helped plant them last spring. How many plants in the garden are NOT sunflowers?

Not-sunflower plants:
✅ Yes — 95 plants. 175 (whole) − 80 (sunflowers) = 95. The "30 students" was extra info.
C
Cora — sense check
"Sense check: the answer should be smaller than the total (175) because not-sunflowers is just a PART of the whole. 95 < 175 ✓ — passes."
🔄 Skip the extra fact. Find the missing part.
Whole garden = 175 plants. One part = 80 sunflowers. Other part = NOT sunflowers = ? Whole − one part = missing part: 175 − 80 = ___. The "30 students" is extra info.
