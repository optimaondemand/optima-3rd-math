Essential Question
Why is 3 × 2 the engine for 3 × 200?

If you can find 3 × 2 = 6, you can find 3 × 20 and 3 × 200. The digits multiply the same way — only the place changes.

✏️ Notice + Wonder
📐 What You Just Saw

The fact is the same. The place changes.

3 × 2 = 6 ones   →   3 × 20 = 6 tens (60)   →   3 × 200 = 6 hundreds (600)

To multiply ones, tens, or hundreds: multiply the digits, then attach the right place. The count of discs stays the same — it just shifts up one place when you go from ones to tens, and again to hundreds.
