Essential Question
How do you compare numbers when the digits look close?

Some numbers are easy to compare. 8,000 is more than 200 — you can see it instantly.

But what about 3,998 and 4,008? The digit 9 looks bigger than 0. So which number is greater?

Concept · Lesson 3.01.03
Compare left to right
🎬
📹 3-01-03_Concept.mp4
Recording prompt: Draw a place-value chart with two rows. Write 3,998 in the top row, 4,008 in the bottom. Point to the thousands column FIRST. "Always start at the left — the biggest place. Thousands: 3 vs 4. They're different. 4 is bigger than 3, so 4,008 is greater. Done. We don't even need to look at the other places." Make it dramatic — circle the 4. "When the leftmost digits are different, the comparison is decided right there. The other digits don't matter, even if they look bigger." 📐 Key Concept — What You Just Saw

To compare two numbers, look at the place-value digits from left to right.

Find the FIRST place where the two digits are different. The number with the bigger digit in that place is the greater number. You don't need to check the places after that — the comparison is already decided.

3,998 < 4,008   ·   4,500 > 4,300   ·   2,617 = 2,617

The open side of > or < always points to the greater number. Some students remember it as "the alligator eats the bigger one."

🔄 Use the clues one at a time to eliminate.
Use each clue to eliminate. Clue 1 wants a 4 in the hundreds place. Clue 2 wants more than 40 hundreds total (review Lesson 1.02 if needed — count the hundreds inside the whole number). Clue 3 wants less than 450 tens (same idea, with tens). Whichever number passes ALL three is the mystery number.
