Essential Question
Close enough — what counts?

An ESTIMATE doesn't have to be exact. It just has to be close enough to be useful. To estimate, you round each number to a friendly place — then do the easy math with the rounded numbers.

The choice you make: which place to round to.

📐 Key Concept

The estimation recipe:

1. Round each number to a friendly place.   2. Add or subtract the rounded numbers.   3. That's your estimate.

Rounding to a SMALLER place (like tens) keeps your estimate close to the actual answer. Rounding to a BIGGER place (like hundreds) is faster but less precise. Pick whichever fits your need.

✅ Yes! 838→800, 72→100, est=700. 584→600, 47→0... wait, 47 rounds to 0 with hundreds! Use tens instead: 584→600, 47→50, est=650 (or even 630). 972→1000, 356→400, est=600. 784→800, 562→600, est=1,400.
C
Cora — checking the work
"When one number is much smaller (like 47), rounding it to hundreds gives 0 — useless! That's a sign to round IT to TENS while still rounding the bigger number to hundreds. Mixing rounding places is a power move."
🔄 Round each number, then compute.
(a) 838→800, 72→100 (or 70 if mixing) — 800−100=700 or 800−70=730.  ·  (b) 584→600, 47→50 — 600+50=650.  ·  (c) 972→1,000, 356→400 — 1,000−400=600.  ·  (d) 784→800, 562→600 — 800+600=1,400.
