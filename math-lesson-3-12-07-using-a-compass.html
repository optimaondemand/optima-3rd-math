📐 Key Concept — What You Just Saw

A compass draws circles by keeping one point fixed (the center) while sweeping the pencil at a set distance (the radius). Every point on the circle is exactly the radius distance from the center — that’s what makes it a perfect circle.

Key fact: diameter = 2 × radius. Radius = diameter ÷ 2.

1
Radius to Diameter
Calculate

You set your compass to 5 cm and draw a circle. What is the diameter of your circle?

Diameter =cm
✅ Radius 5 cm × 2 = diameter 10 cm.
🔄 The diameter is twice the radius. If the radius is 5, what’s 5 × 2?
2
Diameter to Radius
Calculate

You draw a circle. You measure across the widest part: 12 cm. What radius setting did you use?

Radius =cm
✅ Diameter 12 ÷ 2 = radius 6 cm.
🔄 The widest part is the diameter. Radius is half the diameter. What’s 12 ÷ 2?
3
Bigger Circle
Compare

Hazel draws a circle with her compass set to 4 cm. Cora draws a circle with her compass set to 7 cm. Without looking, who drew the bigger circle?

Cora
radius 7 cm
Hazel
radius 4 cm
✅ Bigger radius = bigger circle. 7 > 4, so Cora’s circle is bigger.
🔄 A bigger radius makes a bigger circle. Which radius is bigger: 4 cm or 7 cm?
