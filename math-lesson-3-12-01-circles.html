Vocabulary

Center — the exact middle point of a circle.

Radius — the distance from the center to the edge of a circle.

Diameter — the distance across the circle through the center. Always twice the radius.

Chord — a line segment connecting two points on a circle.

📐 Key Concept — What You Just Saw

A circle is a shape where every point on the edge is the same distance from the center. That distance is the radius. The diameter stretches all the way across through the center. Diameter = 2 × radius.

🧠
Worked Example: Hazel’s Garden
Problem

Radius to Diameter
Find the diameter

A circle has a radius of 5 cm. What is the diameter?

Diameter =cm
✅ 2 × 5 = 10 cm. The diameter is always twice the radius.
🔄 Remember: diameter = 2 × radius. What is 2 × 5?
2
Diameter to Radius
Find the radius

A circle has a diameter of 14 cm. What is the radius?

Radius =cm
✅ 14 ÷ 2 = 7 cm. Half the diameter is the radius.
🔄 The radius is half the diameter. What is half of 14?
3
Which Circle Is Bigger?
Compare

Circle A has a radius of 6 cm. Circle B has a diameter of 10 cm. Which circle is bigger?

Circle A
radius 6 cm
Circle B
diameter 10 cm
✅ Circle A: diameter = 2 × 6 = 12 cm. Circle B: diameter = 10 cm. 12 > 10 — Circle A is bigger.
🔄 Convert both to diameter first. Circle A’s diameter = 2 × 6 = ?. Then compare.
