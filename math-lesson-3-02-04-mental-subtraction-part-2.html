Break-Apart Subtractor
Find 850 − 280 by breaking apart 280.

Watch how the three steps build on each other. Each step takes one chunk away.

850 − 280 = ?
Step 1 — Break apart 280
280 = 200 + 80
280 has 2 hundreds and 8 tens.
Step 2 — Subtract the hundreds
850 − 200 = 650
Take 2 hundreds away from 8 hundreds. 8 − 2 = 6, so 850 − 200 = 650.
Step 3 — Subtract the tens
650 − 80 = 570
From 650, take 50 to land on 600, then take 30 more. 650 − 50 = 600. 600 − 30 = 570.
So 850 − 280 = 570.
✏️ What is 850 − 280? Which step felt the hardest?
📐 Key Concept

To subtract a bigger number in your head:

Break the second number into hundreds and tens. Subtract the hundreds first. Then subtract the tens.

The hundreds part is easy. The tens part takes one more thought — but two easy steps is much faster than one giant step.

📋

Worked Example: 720 − 480
When the tens step crosses a hundred.
Problem

A toy store had 720 marbles. They sold 480 in one week. How many are left? Find 720 − 480 by breaking apart.

A
Atlas — starting
"I start with 720 marbles. I need to subtract 480. First step: I break 480 into hundreds and tens. 480 has 4 hundreds and 8 tens. So 480 = 400 + 80."
H
Hazel — taking the hundreds
"Now I subtract the hundreds first. 720 − 400 means 7 hundreds minus 4 hundreds. That's 3 hundreds, so 720 − 400 = 320. I still need to take away 80."
The three steps:
1️⃣ Break apart: 480 = 400 + 80
2️⃣ Subtract the hundreds: 720 − 400 = 320
3️⃣ Subtract the tens: 320 − 80 = 240
F
Felix — handling the tens
"I'm at 320 and I need to subtract 80. That crosses a hundred — I have to think a bit. I'll take 20 first to land on the friendly number 300. Then I take 60 more. 320 − 20 = 300. 300 − 60 = 240. So 720 − 480 = 240."
C
Cora — checking the work
"Let's check by adding. If 720 − 480 = 240, then 240 + 480 should equal 720. 240 + 400 = 640. 640 + 80 = 720. ✓ It works! 240 marbles left."

720 − 480 = 240

💡

When the tens step crosses a hundred (like 320 − 80), break the tens chunk into TWO smaller pieces — one piece to reach the friendly number, then one piece to finish.
