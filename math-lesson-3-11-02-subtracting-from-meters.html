Essential Question
How do you subtract centimeters from a meter?

You can’t subtract 35 cm from 1 m until you know how many centimeters are in a meter. Convert first, then subtract.

Think About It
🧠

Felix has a 1-meter board. He cuts off 35 cm for a small shelf. How much board is left?

✏️ What would you do first?

Convert first, then cut.

Vocabulary

Remaining — what’s left after you take some away.

Difference — the answer when you subtract. 100 − 35 = 65. The difference is 65.

Subtracting from a meter

Recording prompt: “You have 1 meter of rope. You cut off 35 cm. How much is left? Step 1: convert. 1 m = 100 cm. Step 2: subtract. 100 − 35 = 65 cm. What about 2 m minus 45 cm? Convert: 2 m = 200 cm. 200 − 45 = 155 cm. That’s 1 m 55 cm.” (~2 min)

📐 Key Concept — What You Just Saw

To subtract centimeters from meters: convert the meters to centimeters first (multiply by 100), then subtract. Express your answer in meters and centimeters if it’s 100 or more.
