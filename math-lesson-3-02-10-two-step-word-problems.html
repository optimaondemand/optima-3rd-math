📐 Key Concept

Two-step problems work in two stages:

Step 1: Find the hidden middle number.   Step 2: Use it to answer the real question.

The hidden middle is usually a number the problem TALKS ABOUT but doesn't actually give you. You have to build it from the other two numbers first.

📋
Three Two-Step Problems
Find the middle, then the real answer.

Each problem has a hidden question. Watch how the two steps build on each other.

The first row's hidden step was "find fir." The second row's was "find Felix's cards." The third row's was "find total read so far." The hidden number is always the one the question NEEDS but the problem doesn't give you directly.

Worked Example: The Reading Marathon
A capstone problem using mental math + bar models.
Problem

A class is reading a 600-page book for a marathon. They read 280 pages on Monday and 195 pages on Tuesday. How many pages do they have left to read?

A
Atlas — find the hidden step
"The question is 'how many left.' To find that, I need to know how many they've read so far. But the problem gives me Monday and Tuesday separately. Hidden step: total read so far = 280 + 195."
H
Hazel — solve step 1
"Step 1: 280 + 195. Break apart 195 into 200 − 5. 280 + 200 = 480. Then 480 − 5 = 475. So they've read 475 pages."
F
Felix — solve step 2
"Step 2: how many pages left? Whole − read = left. 600 − 475. Count up from 475: 475 → 500 (+25), 500 → 600 (+100). Total: 125. So 125 pages left."
C
Cora — checking the work
"Let's check that the two pieces add to 600. 475 + 125 → 475 + 100 = 575, then 575 + 25 = 600. ✓ The marathon math holds."

Pages left: 125

💡

Step 1 found a piece the problem didn't hand us (total read so far). Step 2 used that piece to answer the real question. Two steps, in order — and every mental-math tool from this unit showed up along the way.

Word problem

Cora started with 240 cookies. She gave 85 to her class and 57 to her neighbors. How many cookies does Cora have left?

Cookies left:
✅ Yes — 98 cookies. Step 1 (hidden): total given = 85 + 57 = 142. Step 2: left = 240 − 142 = 98.
C
Cora — checking the work
