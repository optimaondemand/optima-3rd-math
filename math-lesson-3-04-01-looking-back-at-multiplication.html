Today's Mission
See multiplication three ways at once — as equal groups, as an array, and as repeated addition.

Essential Question
What is multiplication, really?

Adding 5 + 5 + 5 + 5 = 20 works. But writing it gets long. Multiplication is the shortcut: 4 × 5 = 20.

Today: see why multiplication and repeated addition are the SAME idea — and how an array shows both at once.

📐 Key Concept

Multiplication = equal groups added together.

A × B means "A groups of B" — and also "B groups of A." Same product either way.

An array shows multiplication as ROWS × COLUMNS. Count by rows OR count by columns — you'll get the same total.

📋

From picture to equation to product.
Problem

Each bracelet has 4 charms. How many charms are on 9 bracelets?

A

9 × 4 = 36 charms

C

9 × 4 = 36 charms

💡

Skip-counting and multiplication go together. When you skip-count by 4 nine times, you've calculated 9 × 4.
