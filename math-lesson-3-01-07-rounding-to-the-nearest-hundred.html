📐 Key Concept — What You Just Saw

The rounding rule is the same. Only two things change.

1. The rounding place shifts from thousands to hundreds.
2. The deciding digit shifts one place to the right — from hundreds to tens.

Tens digit 0–4 → round down.  ·  Tens digit 5–9 → round up.

4,820 has tens digit 2 → round down to 4,800. 4,872 has tens digit 7 → round up to 4,900. 4,850 has tens digit 5 → round up to 4,900 (midpoint).

General rule: the deciding digit is always one place to the right of the rounding place.

Worked Example: Mount Tyree, Zoomed In
Round 4,852 to the nearest hundred — four ways.
Problem

Round Mount Tyree's height — 4,852 m — to the nearest hundred. Yesterday this rounded to 5,000 (nearest thousand). What do we get this time?

Watch how each of the Math Crew approaches the same problem:

A
Atlas — concrete
"4,852 has 4 thousands, 8 hundreds, 5 tens, 2 ones. We're rounding to the nearest hundred, so I look at the tens column — there are 5 discs. That's our deciding digit."
H
Hazel — pictorial
"I draw a number line from 4,800 to 4,900 with the midpoint at 4,850. 4,852 sits just past the midpoint — so it's closer to 4,900."
F
Felix — shortcut
"The tens digit is 5. Five is the boundary — round UP. Answer: 4,900. I don't even need the number line."
C
Cora — checking
"Let me check both distances. 4,852 − 4,800 = 52. 4,900 − 4,852 = 48. 48 is less than 52, so 4,900 IS closer. ✓"

4,852 rounded to the nearest hundred is 4,900.

💡

So Mount Tyree is "about 4,900 meters" to the nearest hundred — a more precise headline than "about 5,000 meters."
