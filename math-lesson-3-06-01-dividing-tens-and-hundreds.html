Essential Question
Why is 6 ÷ 3 the engine for 600 ÷ 3?

If you can share 6 ones into 3 rows (2 each), you can share 6 tens or 6 hundreds the same way. The digits divide the same — only the place changes.

✏️ Notice + Wonder
📐 What You Just Saw

The fact is the same. The place changes.

6 ÷ 3 = 2 ones   →   60 ÷ 3 = 2 tens (20)   →   600 ÷ 3 = 2 hundreds (200)

To divide tens or hundreds: divide the digits, then attach the right place. The number of discs in each share stays the same — only the value of each disc shifts up one place.
