What if a number is ALMOST a hundred?

98 is almost 100. 397 is almost 400. 499 is almost 500. Adding 100 is easy. Adding 98? Not as easy — until you cheat a little.

Today: treat the near-hundred as the friendly hundred, then fix the small extra you added.

Concept · Lesson 3.02.06
Round, calculate, adjust
🎬
📹 3-02-06_Concept.mp4
Recording prompt: Walk 174 + 98 cleanly. "Step 1: 98 is 2 away from 100. Step 2: pretend 98 = 100. Now I have 174 + 100 = 274. Easy. Step 3: but I added 2 too many. So I take 2 back: 274 − 2 = 272." Then flip to subtraction: "174 − 98. Same idea. Pretend 98 is 100. 174 − 100 = 74. But I subtracted 2 too many — I took away 2 more than I should have. So I add 2 back: 74 + 2 = 76." Make the contrast clear: "When you ADD too much, take some BACK. When you SUBTRACT too much, give some BACK." (~2 min)

📐 Key Concept

When a number is close to a hundred:

Round it up to the friendly hundred. Do the easy math. Then adjust by the small extra.

For addition, you added too much — so subtract the extra. For subtraction, you took away too much — so add the extra back.

📋
Three Quick Examples
Same recipe — addition and subtraction.

Watch what direction the adjustment goes in each case.

ProblemRound & adjustAnswer
174 − 98Pretend 98 = 100. 174 − 100 = 74. Took 2 too many → add 2 back: 74 + 276
343 + 299Pretend 299 = 300. 343 + 300 = 643. Added 1 too many → subtract 1: 643 − 1642
555 − 96Pretend 96 = 100. 555 − 100 = 455. Took 4 too many → add 4 back: 455 + 4459
🔍

Notice the directions: for ADD, you subtract back. For SUBTRACT, you add back. The trick is always to undo the small extra in the opposite direction.

The three steps:

F
