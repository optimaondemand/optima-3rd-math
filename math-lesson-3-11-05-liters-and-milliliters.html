Vocabulary

Milliliter (mL) — a small unit of liquid volume. About 20 drops of water.

Liter (L) — a larger unit of liquid volume. 1 L = 1,000 mL. A full water bottle.

Capacity — how much liquid a container can hold.

📐 Key Concept — What You Just Saw

1 L = 1,000 mL. To convert liters to milliliters, multiply by 1,000. To find how many liters are in a big number of milliliters, think: “How many thousands?” The leftover is extra milliliters. Just like kilometers and meters!

Convert

1,750 mL = ___ L ___ mL

1,750 mL =LmL
✅ 1,750 has 1 thousand and 750 left over. 1,750 mL = 1 L 750 mL.
🔄 Think: how many groups of 1,000 fit into 1,750? That’s the liters. The leftover is milliliters.
