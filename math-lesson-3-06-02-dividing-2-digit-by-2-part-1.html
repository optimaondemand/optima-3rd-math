Today's Mission
Share a 2-digit number into 2 equal rows when both columns divide cleanly.
❓
Essential Question
Can both columns share evenly?

If the tens digit and the ones digit both divide by 2 without anything left over, the work stays simple. Share each column on its own. Read one row.

Welcome · Lesson 3.06.02
Share 48 into 2 rows
🎬
📹 3-06-02_Welcome.mp4
Recording prompt: Stack 48 on the mat: 4 green tens-discs and 8 orange ones-discs. "I want to split 48 into 2 equal rows." Deal the tens: 2 each. Then deal the ones: 4 each. "Each row has 2 tens and 4 ones — that's 24. So 48 ÷ 2 = 24." Plain voice, no fanfare. (~1–2 min)

Key idea

Share the tens first, then share the ones. When each column has enough discs to share evenly, you're done. Today every problem works out clean — we'll handle leftovers next lesson.

Recording prompt: Build 68 on the mat: 6 tens + 8 ones. Share the tens into 2 rows: 3 each. Share the ones into 2 rows: 4 each. Read: 3 tens + 4 ones = 34. Check: 2 × 34 = 68 ✓. "Even tens digit, even ones digit — splits clean every time." (~2 min)
