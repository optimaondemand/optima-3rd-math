Vocabulary

Equilateral triangle — all 3 sides are equal length.

Isosceles triangle — exactly 2 sides are equal length.

Scalene triangle — all 3 sides are different lengths.

Acute triangle — all angles are less than a right angle.

Obtuse triangle — one angle is greater than a right angle.

Right triangle — one angle is exactly a right angle (a square corner).

📐 Key Concept — What You Just Saw

Triangles are classified two ways. By sides: equilateral (3 equal), isosceles (2 equal), scalene (0 equal). By angles: right (one square corner), acute (all angles less than a right angle), obtuse (one angle greater than a right angle). Every triangle fits one side-type AND one angle-type.

Tricky Thinking
Judgment

Hazel says every equilateral triangle is also isosceles because it has at least 2 equal sides. Is she right?

Yes
No
✅ She’s right! An equilateral triangle has 3 equal sides — which means it definitely has at least 2 equal sides. So it counts as isosceles too.
🔄 Think: if all 3 sides are equal, does it have at least 2 equal sides? Yes! So equilateral triangles ARE isosceles too.
