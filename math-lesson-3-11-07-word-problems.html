Estimate — a quick guess that helps you check if your answer makes sense.

📐 Key Concept — What You Just Saw

Measurement word problems work the same way no matter the unit. Convert to the same unit, do the math, convert back if needed.

Addition — Length
Word Problem

Hazel has a ribbon that’s 1 m 30 cm. She ties on 70 cm more. How long is the ribbon now?

Total length:cm
✅ 1 m 30 cm = 130 cm. 130 + 70 = 200 cm = 2 m.
🎬
Pass · Problem 1

Subtraction — Capacity
Word Problem

Felix has a 3 L jug. He pours out 1,200 mL. How much is left?

Left:LmL
✅ 3 L = 3,000 mL. 3,000 − 1,200 = 1,800 mL = 1 L 800 mL.

Pass · Problem 2

🎬
📹 3-11-07_P2-Pass.mp4

🔄 Convert 3 L to milliliters first. 1 L = 1,000 mL, so 3 L = 3,000 mL. Now subtract 1,200.

Comparison — Mass
Word Problem

Cora’s backpack weighs 2 kg 100 g. Atlas’s weighs 1,850 g. Whose backpack is heavier? By how many grams?

Who has the heavier backpack?

Cora
2 kg 100 g
Atlas
1,850 g
Difference:g
✅ Cora: 2 kg 100 g = 2,100 g. Atlas: 1,850 g. 2,100 − 1,850 = 250 g heavier.
🎬
Pass · Problem 3
Compare by converting
🎬
📹 3-11-07_P3-Pass.mp4
Recording prompt: “Convert both to grams. 2 kg 100 g = 2,100 g. Atlas has 1,850 g. 2,100 − 1,850 = 250 g. Cora’s is heavier by 250 g.” (~30 sec)
🔄 Convert Cora’s backpack: 2 kg = 2,000 g, plus 100 g = 2,100 g. Now subtract Atlas’s 1,850 g.
