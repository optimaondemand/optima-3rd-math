📐 Key Concept

The value of a digit depends on its place.

In 4,326, the digit 4 is worth 4,000 because it is in the thousands place. The digit 4 does not mean 4 ones here.

Worked Example
Read, build, write.
Problem

Build and write the number with 5 thousands, 1 hundred, 7 tens, and 9 ones.

Watch how each of the Math Crew approaches building 5,179:

A
Atlas — concrete
"Put 5 thousand-discs, 1 hundred-disc, 7 ten-discs, and 9 one-discs on the mat. Each disc lands in its own column."
H
Hazel — pictorial
"Drop them into the place-value chart: thousands 5, hundreds 1, tens 7, ones 9. Reading left to right gives 5,179."

5,179 = 5,000 + 100 + 70 + 9

F
Felix — shortcut
"Skip the discs once you see the pattern. The digit and its place tell you the value: 5 in thousands is 5,000."
C
Cora — checking
"Add up the expanded form: 5,000 + 100 + 70 + 9 = 5,179. ✓ Same number, two ways to write it."
💡

Notice: every place has a digit, so no zero is needed. The chart shows it all at once.
