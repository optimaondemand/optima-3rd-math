Essential Question
How do you find the rule of a number pattern?

A pattern is a row of numbers where each one follows the same rule. Once you find the rule, the rest is easy.

Today's rules are simple: add 10, subtract 100, add 1,000, and so on.

📐 Key Concept — What You Just Saw

To find the rule of a pattern, compare any two numbers that are next to each other.

The difference between them is the rule. If the second is bigger, the rule adds. If smaller, it subtracts. Once you know the rule, apply it to find the next number — or work backward to find an earlier one.

3,200 → 3,210 → 3,220 → 3,230  ·  Rule: +10

A pattern is just skip-counting with a rule. Find the rule, find the next number.

📋
Three Patterns, Three Rules
Different rules. Same idea.

Read each row. The rule tells you what changes between numbers. Notice how the digits in different places move.

PatternCompare two termsRule
2,608 · 2,708 · 2,808 · 2,908Hundreds digit goes up by 1 each step.+100
1,091 · 1,081 · 1,071 · 1,061Tens digit goes down by 1 each step.−10
5,893 · 6,893 · 7,893 · 8,893Thousands digit goes up by 1 each step.+1,000
🔍

Look for the pattern in the pattern: The rule tells you which place changes. +10 changes tens. +100 changes hundreds. +1,000 changes thousands. (Watch out for "carries" — sometimes adding 10 also changes hundreds, like 1,090 + 10 = 1,100.)

A Saving Pattern
Numbers in real life.
🔍

What changed? Now the pattern is hidden inside a story. You'll have to write the pattern yourself.

Word problem

Maya saves $100 each week. On Week 1 she has $100 saved. On Week 2 she has $200. On Week 3 she has $300.

What is the rule? How much will Maya have on Week 7?

Rule: +each week
Week 7 total: $
✅ Yes! Rule: +100 per week. Week 7: 7 × $100 = $700. Or skip-count: $100, $200, $300, $400, $500, $600, $700.
🔄 Walk through the weeks one at a time.
Week 1 = $100. Each week she adds $100 more. Week 2 = $200, Week 3 = $300… keep going until Week 7.
