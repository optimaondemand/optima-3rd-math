Today's Mission
Split a total into equal groups — either by picking how many groups OR how many in each group.
❓
Essential Question
What does ÷ really mean?

Division splits a total into equal parts. You can split by NUMBER OF GROUPS ("12 ÷ 4" = put 12 into 4 groups, find group size) or by GROUP SIZE ("12 ÷ 3" = make groups of 3, find how many fit).

Two meanings, one operation.

Think About It: Two Meanings
🧠

Both problems use 12 counters and the answer comes from division. But they ASK different questions.

Problem A: "Divide 12 counters into 4 equal groups. How many in each group?"
Problem B: "Divide 12 counters into groups of 3. How many groups?"

Both are division. Both give an answer of … well, that's the question. What's the answer to each?

📐 Key Concept

Two meanings of ÷:

Share: total ÷ number-of-groups = how many in each.   Measure: total ÷ group-size = how many groups fit.

Use a related multiplication fact to find the quotient: if 4 × 3 = 12, then 12 ÷ 4 = 3 and 12 ÷ 3 = 4.
