Today's Mission
Use a known fact to find a harder one — by splitting one factor into smaller, easier pieces.

Essential Question
Stuck on a fact? Split it.

6 × 7 feels harder than 6 × 5. But you can break the 7 into 5 + 2, then add: 6 × 5 + 6 × 2 = 30 + 12 = 42.

Today: turn a hard multiplication into two easy ones.

📐 Key Concept

The split-and-add strategy:

A × B = A × (smaller + smaller) = (A × smaller) + (A × smaller).

Split a hard factor into pieces you already know (like 5s, 2s, or 10s). Multiply each piece separately. Add the partial products. That's it.

📋

Worked Example: 7 × 8
A tough one made easy.
Problem

Find 7 × 8. Use a known fact to help you.

A
Atlas — pick a known fact
"I know 5 × 8 = 40. I'll split 7 into 5 + 2. Then 7 × 8 = (5 × 8) + (2 × 8)."
H
Hazel — find the pieces
"5 × 8 = 40. 2 × 8 = 16. Add them: 40 + 16 = 56. So 7 × 8 = 56."
7 × 8 = (5 × 8) + (2 × 8) = 40 + 16 = 56
F
Felix — try doubling
"Another way: I know 7 × 4 = 28. Double it for 7 × 8 = 56. ✓ Same answer."
C
Cora — checking
"Two paths, same product. That's how you know your answer is right."
