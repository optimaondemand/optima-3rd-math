Vocabulary

A common denominator is the same bottom number for two fractions. To compare fractions with different bottoms, re-cut both into the same number of pieces, then count the shaded ones. 23 = 812 and 34 = 912. Now compare 8 and 9. The strip with 9 shaded is bigger.

Worked Example: Compare 2/3 and 3/4
Problem

Hazel has 23 of a candy bar. Atlas has 34 of the same-size bar. Who has more?

A
Atlas — different bottoms
"Thirds and fourths are different sizes. I can't just count."
H
Hazel — find a common bottom
"3 × 4 = 12. Both can go to 12ths. 2/3 = 8/12 and 3/4 = 9/12."
C
Cora — compare tops
"Same bottom. 9 > 8. Atlas has more."

34  >  23  (because 9/12 > 8/12)
