Tens, hundreds, or thousands?

With bigger numbers comes a bigger choice. Should you round 4,223 to the nearest thousand (4,000) or the nearest hundred (4,200)? Each gives a different estimate — and a different level of speed and accuracy.

Today: pick the rounding place that fits the question.

📐 Key Concept

Estimating with bigger numbers:

Round each number to the place that fits your need. Round to a SMALLER place (tens or hundreds) for a closer estimate. Round to a BIGGER place (thousands) for faster math.

When the numbers are very different sizes (like $2,413 and $450), you can round each one to a DIFFERENT place. The big one to thousands or hundreds. The small one to tens or hundreds. That's a power move.

🔍

For 4-digit numbers, rounding to HUNDREDS usually gives a really good estimate. Rounding to THOUSANDS is faster but can be off by more.

Answer: 4,299 rounds to 4,000. 3,467 rounds to 3,000. Estimate: 4,000 + 3,000 = 7,000. But actual is 7,766 — actually closer to 8,000!
