Today's Mission
Multiply a 2-digit number by 1 digit using the place-value mat. No regrouping today.
❓
Essential Question
Why do columns matter?

If you build the number out of tens and ones, you can multiply each part by itself. The mat keeps the parts in their own columns so you don't mix them up.

📐 What You Just Saw

The mat keeps the parts separate.

Multiply ones × multiplier → put in the Ones column.
Multiply tens × multiplier → put in the Tens column.
Read the total: tens + ones.

Today there were never 10 or more discs in a column. That means no trades — no regrouping. Next lesson we'll see what to do when a column overflows.
