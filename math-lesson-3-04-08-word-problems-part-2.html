Today's Mission
Solve "3 times as many" problems using stacked comparison bars.

Essential Question
How do bars compare?

When one amount is "3 times as many" as another, you stack 3 equal bars to match it. The total of both is 4 units.

📐 Key Concept

The unit method: Count all the units across both bars. Divide the total by the number of units to find ONE unit. Then multiply to find any bar.

Total ÷ total units = value of one unit

📋
Example: Pen + Notebook = $12
StepWhat we do
1Pen = 1 unit, notebook = 3 units. Total = 4 units.
24 units = $12. So 1 unit = $12 ÷ 4 = $3.
3Pen = 1 unit = $3. Notebook = 3 units = $9.

Problem

A farmer has 5 times as many sheep as goats. He has 40 more sheep than goats. How many goats are there?

A

"Goats = 1 unit. Sheep = 5 units. The difference is 5 − 1 = 4 units, and that difference equals 40."
H

"4 units = 40, so 1 unit = 40 ÷ 4 = 10. Goats = 10."
