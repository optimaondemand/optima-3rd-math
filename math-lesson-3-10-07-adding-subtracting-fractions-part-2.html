✏️ How do you know when a word problem means “add” vs “subtract”?

Read the story. Pick the operation. Do the math.

Vocabulary

Combining (addition clue words) — “in all,” “total,” “altogether,” “combined.” These signal addition.

Taking away (subtraction clue words) — “left,” “remaining,” “ate,” “gave away,” “how much more.” These signal subtraction.

📐 Key Concept

Read the problem. Look for clue words — “total” and “altogether” mean add; “left” and “remaining” mean subtract. Same bottom → add or subtract the tops. Keep the bottom.

Worked Example: Felix’s Granola Bar
Problem

Felix has 68 of a granola bar. He gives 28 to Cora. Then Atlas gives Felix 18 more. How much does Felix have now?

“Then Atlas gives me 18 more — add. 48 + 18 = 58.”

“Felix ends up with 58. The bottom stayed 8 the whole time.”
