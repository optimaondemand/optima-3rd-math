Today's Mission
Take a four-digit number and find the nearest thousand — by deciding which side of the midpoint it lives on.
❓
Essential Question
How do you decide which thousand a number is closest to?

Numbers don't always come in clean, round amounts. Mount Tyree is 4,852 m. The biggest meteorite crater in the U.S. is 4,150 feet across.

Sometimes we just want the closest thousand — an estimate that's easier to remember. Today you'll learn the rule.

📐 Key Concept — What You Just Saw

Every four-digit number sits between two thousands. 4,852 lives between 4,000 and 5,000. The midpoint between them is 4,500.

Below the midpoint → round down.  ·  At the midpoint or above → round up.

4,200 is below 4,500 → rounds DOWN to 4,000. 4,852 is above 4,500 → rounds UP to 5,000. 4,500 is exactly at the midpoint → rounds UP to 5,000 (the standard rule).

Shortcut: look at the hundreds digit. If it's less than 5, round down. If it's 5 or more, round up.

📋

🔍

The hundreds digit is the shortcut: if it's 0, 1, 2, 3, or 4 → round down. If it's 5, 6, 7, 8, or 9 → round up. That's why mathematicians say "look at the next place to the right of the rounding place."
